## How many people should a manager manage?

* [Trend in larger companies towards larger teams](https://apple.news/AriPg99wtSOq3UN3wIdyxfg). In 2026 many larger companies were betting that AI can magically allow for a doubling in team size.
* [Bob Sutton on team size](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7472705028496150528/). Commentary on [Meta's disasterous attempt to increase team size](https://www.wired.com/story/andrew-bosworth-meta-employees-unrest/). Routine work can have larger teams. Moving to larger teams requires clear rules, norms and goals.
* [There are real tradeoffs](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7477051240498110464/) by me. Larger teams are desirable because they flatten corporate hierarchy. But the cost is local effectiveness and retention. Also smaller teams are more innovative.
