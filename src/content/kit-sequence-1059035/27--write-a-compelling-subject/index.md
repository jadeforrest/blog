​

This is a pretty deep topic. First of all, you have to be able to identify the areas they need to grow in. I try to periodically go through all the people on my team and come up with a sentence that represents my advice to them on how to be more impactful. For example, “you need to learn how to listen as well as you speak”. It has to really get at the heart of things, so I often have to fiddle with it until it seems right.

​

Then, you have to get them to see that it’s a valuable thing to focus on. My favorite approach here is to give them observations — things you’ve noticed. It’s hard to argue with observations. Tie it to the impact they had (either by making mistakes or by not having the skill be better developed).

​

Then you have to coach them on it. I like to think of the next stage as training them. If you think of yourself as responsible for them being better at this skill, what approaches can you take? You can give them assignments, give them feedback, etc.
