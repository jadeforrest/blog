Oh my god the meetings!

​

Pay attention to the cycle time on decisions and discussions.

Complex topics should use meetings, unless you're in a completely async culture. (In that case, you should have patterns to follow)

Color code things.

A few rules for calendars:

\- never allow multiple meetings at the same time. If you get a conflict, reject one of the meetings, or move it.

-   color code your meetings
-   Use invite status to convey intent: yes, attending. maybe attending. No, not attending.
-   Block out time for yourself. Lunches, walks, personal appointments. I like Reclaim for scheduling these automatically, and blocking out time that is on my personal calendar from work.
-   Don't let your calendar get full, because then people will schedule time with you in order to meet with you. If there isn't time in the next couple of days for someone to meet with you, you have a problem. You need to say no to something, defer something, or you're too busy.
-   Being too defensive with your calendar can thus cause problems. Again, I like reclaim because it blocks things off for you, but does it in a flexible way that works around other things that are scheduled.

​

Later, you'll learn some techniques for reducing meetings.
