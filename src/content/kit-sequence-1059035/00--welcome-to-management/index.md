Hello there! Welcome to my course on getting started as an engineering manager. I'll use the term "front-line" managers for your role. Front-line managers are managers that are directly manage a team, instead of being a manager of managers.

## New manager, or not?

You may have been a front-line manager for a while. Or this might be your first day as a manager. This course is for you either way.

## New managers

Congratulations on your move to management! Management and leadership can be tremendously rewarding work. But it’s also a completely new type of work you’re embarking on.

Be sure to talk through the topics we cover with your manager. Every company is different, and there may be some things I recommend that you want to do differently at your company.

## More experienced managers

I had a wonderful manager named Alex once. He asked me a question once during a one on one. He said, “if someone came in and took your job tomorrow, what do you think they would focus on?”

I like to ask myself that question about once a month. What would I do if I was new here, and trying to prove myself all over again?

It can be helpful to approach your role with “beginner’s eyes”. So, with that in mind, pretend you’re starting your role for the first time. Some of the topics I cover may seem basic. But I will be introducing mental models that you may find useful. And, you can think about how you would coach other managers as they’re starting.

## How frequently will I get courses?

You'll get the first email tomorrow, and after that, you'll get an email every Monday morning. The posts will continue for four months, and then you'll get to the end of the courses I've already written.

I may decide to add posts to this course in the future. This means after the course wraps up, you may get posts every once in a while. But they will be much less frequent.

## What topics will we cover?

1.  We’ll review what you’re responsible for. (This will come tomorrow or next business day)
2.  We'll set up your 1-1s with your team, and go over how to run them.
3.  We'll set up your support network.
4.  We'll talk about how to make sure you let go of some of your old responsibilities.

In the future, topics include:

-   Running retrospectives
-   Retention reviews
-   Demos
-   User stories
-   Project plans
-   Technical plans
-   Solving org problems
-   Compensation
-   Role definition
-   Shit shielding or not

and a few more topics.
